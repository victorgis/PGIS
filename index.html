Introduction

Precise GIS is a Geo-spatial company that is based in Akwa Ibom State. A company with premium interest in the collection, manipulation, management, analysis and presentation of spatial data to facilitate decision making. With the aid of Geographic Information System (GIS), Precise-GIS puts together layers of information from different sources to helps visualize pattern and changes in developmental trends. It is the result of this effort that precipitates into a deep understanding of relationships between various actors and actions; situations and processes in our environment thereby helping users to make smart decisions. It is a reality that every business, social event or transaction happens at a specific location, for reasons that, when clearly understood, comprise the “intelligence” component of Location Intelligence (LI). As a result, Maps (a diagrammatic presentation of spatial information) are becoming the language in which to present data, exposing spatial relationships and influences that just aren’t visible in traditional tabular views of data.

The New Insight

This new insight explains where a problem or opportunity exists and why. Location intelligence is defined as the capacity to organize and understand complex relationships and interactions occurring within a geographical sphere and the ability to predict into future events. LI organizes social and geographically referenced data to reveal the relationship of location to people, events, transactions, facilities, and assets. It is observed that the need for this sort of intelligence is more pressing than ever. There are location and geographic contexts to every element of activity, and organizations overlook it at their peril. LI presents a significant opportunity for organizations in nearly every industry to leverage location data as part of their everyday decision-making process. Any analysis relating to social, economic processes, such service and supply chain optimization, risk management, and operational efficiency, benefits when location and any type of data is combine to make sound data-driven decisions.

What is Spatial Analysis

Spatial analysis refers to the "ability to manipulate spatial data into different forms and extract additional meaning as a result. It encompasses the many methods and procedures, developed in geography, statistics, and other disciplines, for analyzing and relating spatial information. Spatial relationships, those based on proximity and relative location, form the core of spatial analysis.

What is Image Classification

Image classification refers to the task of extracting information classes from a multiband raster image. The resulting raster from image classification can be used to create thematic maps. Depending on the interaction between the analyst and the computer during classification, there are two types of classification: supervised and unsupervised.
